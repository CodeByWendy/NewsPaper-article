Startup Spotlight: The Rise of Women in Tech

From coding bootcamps to leadership roles, women are breaking barriers in South Africa’s growing tech industry. Initiatives like GirlCode and SheCodes are leading the change. These initiatives are crucial in adressing the gender imbalance in the tech industry. By providing training, mentorship and supporting women with the skills needed to succed in a rapidly growning field

“When women are included in technology, innovation becomes more creative, solutions are more inclusive, and communities thrive,” says GirlCode founder Zandile Mkwanazi.

The impact of these efforts is already visible. According to recent studies, the number of women enrolling in coding bootcamps and computer science programs in South Africa has increased significantly in the past five years. More local startups are being founded or co-founded by women, and major tech companies are recognising the value of diverse teams that better reflect the communities they serve.

Education: FNB App Academy Expands Its Developer Program

The FNB App Academy continues to empower young software developers with full-stack skills, preparing them for high-impact roles in the tech industry. Established by First National Bank (FNB), the academy bridges the gap between traditional education and real-world experience, offering hands on training in coding, app development, and agile teamwork.

Through its immersive program, participants work on live digital projects that contribute directly to FNB’s innovation goals. This practical approach ensures that graduates not only understand technology but can also apply their skills to solve real business challenges.

Beyond technical training, the academy plays a vital role in addressing South Africa’s digital skills shortage. By providing young people from diverse backgrounds with industry ready expertise, FNB is helping to open pathways to employment and entrepreneurship in the fast paced tech sector. The success of the App Academy demonstrates how corporate initiatives can make a lasting impact.
